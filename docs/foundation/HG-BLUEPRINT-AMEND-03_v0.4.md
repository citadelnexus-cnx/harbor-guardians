# HG-BLUEPRINT-AMEND-03 (v0.4)
## Art Direction & Combat Inspiration Boundary

## A3.1 Art & RPG Inspiration Doctrine
Harbor Guardians may draw **pattern-level** inspiration from classic cinematic JRPGs, including The Legend of Dragoon: active turn-based execution, transformation spectacle, elemental identity, ornate fantasy silhouettes, high-drama battle presentation. The *combination of patterns* is the reference — never any asset, term, or story — filtered through original identity: fortified harbor worlds, animal guardians, Tide-based combat, settlement-as-character progression, contribution-earned Merit.
Pattern → original translation: timed attack engagement → **Tide Chain** (D4) · accumulated battle charge → **Bond Charge** (D5) · battle-earned transformation payoff → **Guardian Surge** (D9) · elemental spectacle → six-region elemental wheel + guardian identity · dramatic JRPG staging → readable harbor/raid/exploration arenas. Relationship: **Tide Chain builds Bond Charge; Bond Charge powers Guardian Surge.**

## A3.2 Art Direction — **Mythic Coastal JRPG Fantasy** (D8)
Definition: fortified harbors, elemental coastlines, readable silhouettes, guardian-scale spectacle, painterly world spaces, luminous transformation moments, tactical battle clarity. World mood: painterly, high-contrast, mystical coastlines, sacred harbors, elemental regions. Characters: readable silhouettes, distinctive color identity, ornate-but-functional gear. Guardians: majestic animal spirits with settlement-scale presence, never pet mascots. Transformations: luminous resonance states; wing/halo/rune/crest motifs; no imitated dragon-armor forms. UI: dark + gold brand chrome, readable tactical overlays, no clutter. Battle staging: dramatic camera beats, clear turn bar, readable hit timing, strong impact frames. Harbor view: fortified city as character sheet — walls, workers, visible surplus yards, docks, sanctum, guardian presence.

## A3.3 Combat Accessibility Doctrine (D10 — mandatory from first prototype)
Base attack always resolves; timing only adds bonus; no progression gate requires manual precision timing. From the first prototype: assist mode · auto-complete at reduced bonus · tunable timing windows · colorblind-safe cues (shape+icon+text, never color-only) · no audio-only cues · touch-first mobile input · reduced-effects mode · fatigue-friendly auto-resolve · **training dummy/practice mode before balance tuning** · **input-latency calibration before any public playable claim**.

## A3.4 Art-reference production workflow (operationalized boundary)
**Forbidden:** direct image-to-image generation from Legend of Dragoon screenshots, official art, fan art, or manuals; tracing armor, wings, helmets, UI timing markers, character poses, or transformation sequences; Dragoon names/terms, Addition terminology, Spirit-Point terminology, winged-armor silhouettes, timing-ring UI, character compositions, scene layouts, logos, fonts, story beats, trade dress.
**Allowed pattern notes only** (reference boards pattern-labeled, never asset-labeled): mythic transformation spectacle · timed-execution tension · readable battle staging · elemental identity · dramatic pre-rendered-era fantasy mood.
**Every asset carries provenance:** generated / original sketch / commissioned / public-domain / reference-notes. A missing or failed provenance entry blocks the asset.

## A3.5 Safety doctrine (art/combat scope)
No real-money gambling · no paid power · no dark-pattern timer skips · no forced streaks · no hidden offline punishment · no irreversible failure in normal play · no loss without ledger receipt · no accessibility-hostile timing requirement. Every borrowed convention carries ≥1 original twist.
